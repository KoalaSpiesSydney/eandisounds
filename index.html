/iː/ Long “ee” sound

/iː/— long “ee” is easy and common for most native speakers but many language only have the shortened version.

Commonly mispronounced; evening (EEvening), beach (bEEch)

This is a voiced sound so it is produced in the back of your throat with no or little air escaping your lips. However, and most importantly you must show your teeth and smile to shape the sound and hold it so that it is long.

Common spelling of /iː/ ; 'ee' and 'ene'. Words like 'scene' and 'seen' are pronounced the same. 'ea' is often pronounced this way, for instance in 'sea' or 'team' or 'beak' but not always, for instance 'steak' /aɪ/ (see below).

/ɪ/ Short “i” sound

/ɪ/ — this is a short sound very common in many languages. It is the same sound in Spanish, Japanese and many others.

/ɪ/ This is a voiced sound so it is produced in the back of your throat with no or little air escaping your lips. The sound is short and pushed to the roof of your mouth. You shouldn't see any teeth when you pronounce it and it is short- so don't hold it.

/ɛ/ Short 'e' sound

/ɛ/ — this is a also short sound and the same as the vowel sound in most phonetic language from Spanish to Japanese

/ɛ/ This is a voiced sound so it is produced in the back of your throat with no or little air escaping your lips. The sound is short and pushed to the bottom of your mouth. You might lower your jaw a little. Your tongue is in the middle‑front of your mouth, and your lips stay relaxed

/aɪ/ Long/Open “eye” sound

/aɪ/ — this is a dipthong - to vowel sounds run together that make one, what is usually called long but I like to think of as open sound. It is often the same sound with spelling varietions 'ae', 'ei' or 'y'

/aɪ/ This is a voice sound produced at the back of the throat and you need to use your lower jaw to push it out. Americans who speak from the front of their mouth might show teeth, Aussies who speak from the middle of their mouth are famous for singing this vowel sound.

/eɪ/ Long/Open “ay” sound

/eɪ/ — “hey or hay”

/eɪ/ is the short “e” sound, like in bed, pen, or many. This is a voice sound pushed out from the back of your throat. This is the most open sound, you need to use your top and bottom jaw, and push the sound out throw the top of your mouth, and your lips stay relaxed

Hack- most of the time (but not all of the time) if there is an 'e' at the end of the word that is not pronounced, the vowel in the middle will be long. In the case of a + e and e + e this will be the same ei sound. Also 'ight' is pronounced 'ite.

Consider these words with different spelling but the same sound: seen & scene / stake & steak / site, cite & sight / write & right
